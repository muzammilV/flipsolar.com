Solar Panels for Restaurants in Texas:
The Complete 2025 Guide

☀️ FlipSolar Editorial Team 📅 Updated June 2025 ⏱️ 8 min read 🤠 Texas

If you own or operate a restaurant in Texas, electricity is almost certainly one of your top three operating costs. Between commercial HVAC, kitchen equipment, refrigeration, and lighting, a mid-sized Texas restaurant burns through $2,000–$5,000 in electricity every single month.

Solar panels can cut that bill by 40–70%. And with the 30% federal tax credit still available through 2032, there has never been a better time for Texas restaurant owners to make the switch.

This guide covers everything — real cost numbers, realistic savings, the tax credit, payback timeline, and how to avoid the installers who will waste your time.

💡 Bottom line upfront: A Texas restaurant paying $3,000/month in electricity will typically save $1,200–$1,800/month after going solar, with a full payback in 3–5 years and 20+ years of near-free electricity after that.

Why Texas Restaurants Are Ideal Solar Candidates

Texas is the #1 state in the country for new solar capacity added in 2025. There are three reasons restaurants specifically are such strong solar candidates:

  • High daytime energy consumption. Restaurants run heavy loads during daylight hours — exactly when solar produces the most power. Your lunch and dinner prep, HVAC and refrigeration all run while the sun is up.
  • Large roof or parking area. Most Texas restaurant buildings have flat commercial roofs or adjacent land ideal for a ground-mounted array.
  • Rising electricity rates. ERCOT commercial rates have risen 18% over the past 3 years. Solar locks in your energy cost before the next hike.

How Much Does Solar Cost for a Texas Restaurant?

System cost depends on your monthly electricity consumption. Here is a real breakdown by restaurant size:

  • Small café / QSR (bill: $1,000–$2,000/mo): System size 20–40kW · Cost before incentives: $40,000–$80,000 · After 30% ITC: $28,000–$56,000
  • Mid-size restaurant (bill: $2,000–$4,000/mo): System size 40–80kW · Cost before incentives: $80,000–$160,000 · After 30% ITC: $56,000–$112,000
  • Large restaurant / chain (bill: $4,000–$8,000/mo): System size 80–160kW · Cost before incentives: $160,000–$320,000 · After 30% ITC: $112,000–$224,000

📌 Important: These are pre-financing numbers. Many Texas restaurants finance solar with $0 down through a solar loan, meaning they pay less per month than their current electricity bill from day one — with no capital outlay.

The 30% Federal Investment Tax Credit (ITC)

  • Your business gets a 30% tax credit on the total installed cost — not a deduction, a direct credit against your tax bill.
  • If your system costs $100,000, you get $30,000 back from the IRS.
  • Texas has no state income tax, so every dollar of federal savings goes straight to your bottom line.
  • The ITC is available through 2032 at the full 30% rate. It steps down after that.
  • You can also use bonus depreciation to write off up to 60% of the remaining system cost in year one.

Real Savings: What Texas Restaurants Actually Save

  • Monthly electricity bill before solar: $3,000
  • Solar system size: 60kW · System cost before ITC: $120,000
  • After 30% ITC: $84,000 net cost
  • Monthly bill after solar: $600–$900 (remaining grid usage at night)
  • Monthly savings: $2,100–$2,400 · Annual savings: $25,000–$29,000
  • Full payback period: 3–4 years · 25-year total savings: $500,000+

Solar Financing Options for Texas Restaurants

  1. Cash purchase. Best long-term ROI. You own the system outright and claim the full ITC. Payback in 3–5 years, then 20+ years of near-free power.
  2. Solar loan ($0 down). Most popular option. Monthly loan payment is less than your current electricity bill — immediate net savings with no capital outlay.
  3. Solar lease / PPA. Fixed monthly rate for power generated. No ITC benefit but no maintenance responsibility. Good for restaurants with thin capital.
  4. PACE financing. Property Assessed Clean Energy — attached to your property taxes, not your credit. Available in many Texas counties.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does installation take?

Most commercial installations in Texas take 2–4 weeks from permit approval to final inspection. Total timeline from quote to live system: 2–4 months.

Will solar panels affect my restaurant's operations?

Minimal disruption. Good installers schedule rooftop work during off-hours. The grid connection switch-over typically takes just 2–4 hours.

What happens on cloudy days or at night?

Your restaurant stays connected to the ERCOT grid and draws power normally. You only pay for what solar doesn't cover. Many restaurants add a battery backup to reduce reliance on peak-rate grid power.

Can I get solar if I lease my restaurant building?

It is possible but requires landlord cooperation. The best approach is a Solar Rider Agreement attached to your lease. Get 3 quotes first so you know the numbers before approaching your landlord.
